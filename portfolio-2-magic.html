Magic

Advanced Software Design

Description

2018 Spring

Software Development (Team of 3)

Programmer / UX Design / Video Editor

Process Analysis

The impetus behind MAGIC came from the surprising lack of tools supporting the active, simultaneous development of multiple, alternative solutions. Artists typically begin their work with traditional pen and paper, which is sufficient for the quick recording of rough ideas. However, intermediary and more polished sketches are better created digitally, where computational tools offer the benefits of quick duplication and manipulation. While some experimental tools do explore the possibility of simultaneous exploration, there are currently none which analyze the contents of the drawing space. We propose a new tool, MAGIC (Make and Generate Intelligent Combinations) that intelligently recognizes the composition of subjects being sketched, and will retrieve sketches of common objects based on labels defined by the user, system-detected general shapes, as well as the user’s drawing input. 

The workhorse of MAGIC will be its combination generation function, which allows artists to quickly combine multiple sketches and preview combinations, which are retrieved via user-defined hierarchy trees. Like preceding tools, MAGIC will also support the development of one or multiple sketches simultaneously, offer variation-independent history keeping, and provide means to categorize and organize sketches based on labels and annotations. 

The MAGIC is still on the alpha stage which I believe it has the potential to be developed further. One of the challenge we keep struggling during the design phase is whether make the team collaboration as the major function of the software, we find that most creative support tools support team collaboration, such as Google Docs. In order to dig this problem further, we have interviewed with many artists, and we come up the final solution. Because our targeted users are game artists, or those artists tasked with creating the visual assets for environments, game objects, and character designs. Although they are often part of larger game design teams, they usually do the bulk of their work alone. Thus, team collaboration in MAGIC is not a priority at this time.
