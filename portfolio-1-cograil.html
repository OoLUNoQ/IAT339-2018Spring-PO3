Process Analysis

Cograils is a 2d sidescroller, platformer and puzzle-solving game. It is about a loose cog in a clock (the player) that aims to return to its place in the network of cogs by solving a series of puzzles and challenges over 5 levels. The setting of the game is inside a clock, but the artwork and theme of the game interpret the inner workings of a clock as a map of paths and puzzles. The game is heavily movement-centric, limiting the cog to moving along rails in certain areas of the game world. Additional feature like power-ups are available in last two levels to aid the player in solving difficult challenges.

Cograils is designed to target a very broad audience of casual gamers regardless of age and gender. Based on Bartleâ€™s player types, the players of the game do not fall into any clear category due to the overly simplified model of player types. Rather, we are targeting a group of players which mostly achievers and explorers.

In order to make the game more realistic, I try to implement the real-world physics into the programming, such as the cogs will be affected by the gravity. Main problem I find during the programming is the Unity physic library has lots of conflicts with other libraries, so I need to re-design and programming my own physics library. Based on the game concept, I designed and programmed the physics includes gravity, bounce effect, acceleration, and centripetal force. By user testing, we received a lot of positive feedbacks about the physics system I have designed.
